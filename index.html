Shiqi Zhang (章仕起)

I am a Ph.D. candidate in Electrical Engineering at Yanshan University. My research focuses on power electronics and renewable-to-hydrogen systems, including electrolyzer power supplies, multi-port DC–DC converter topologies, impedance modeling & stability under weak grids, and reliability-aware control.

I work on modeling, control, optimization, and demonstration-oriented prototyping for photovoltaic/wind-powered hydrogen production, with particular interest in low-ripple, high power-density converter design and AI/DRL-based energy management.

Research Interests
  • • Electrolyzer power electronics & low-ripple DC–DC converters
  • • Multi-port converters (TAB/DAB/Buck/Boost families) for renewable-to-hydrogen
  • • Impedance modeling & stability analysis in weak-grid / converter-dominated systems
  • • Reliability-aware control, fault diagnosis, and fault-tolerant operation
  • • Optimization & AI/DRL-based energy management for PV/wind-to-hydrogen microgrids
